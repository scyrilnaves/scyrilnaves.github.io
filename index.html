I am a Smart Mobility Engineer. I finished my PhD Research 2023 in Computer Science at University Cote D'Azur in Laboratoire d'Electronique, Antennes et Télécommunications jointly with Renault Software Factory. I work with the EDGE Team focused on Edge Computing and Digital systems.

My dissertation is Distributed Ledger Technology enabled New Generation Mobility in a joint project between Renault & University spearheading its Digital transformation strategy.

I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Francois Verdier. I am also extremely grateful to be co-advised Industriallly by my principal advisor Severine Glock and advisor Patricia Guitton of Mobilize and Renault Software Factory.

Previously In 2019, I received my Masters in Computer Science from University Cote D'Azur where I was directed by David Bercovitz working on Digital Transformation of the Automotive Industry by Blockchain Technology at Renault Software Factory.

In detail, my current research interests are:
  • To study the applicability of a blockchain / DLT solution to several families of connected and autonomous vehicle use cases, namely: DaaS (Data as a Service) and MaaS (Mobility as a Service) and derive the associated requirements.
  • To make an updated inventory of the various Blockchains / DLTs available today, to study their specificities and differences by focusing on their consensus algorithm and the associated governance.
  • To study the applicability of these technologies to the different use cases listed below and to deduce the requirements and impacts to be applied to our vehicles: 1. The reporting of car usage data, for example mileage by eventually creating a Car Data Marketplace where the data could be monetized. The targeted data could come from vehicle data, V2X data and / or user data (IVI).
  • Our subject being at the level of a private blockchain or consortium, it will be necessary to respond to scientific obstacles such as the embeddable aspects of a DLT node on a car (energy, real time, possible partitioning of the algorithm, resynchronization of the on-board node depending on whether or not there is connectivity, etc.), interoperability, etc
